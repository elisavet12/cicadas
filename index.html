Cicadas Life Cycle

  • They have been waiting 17 years under the trees.
  • On a warm spring night their wait comes to an end
  • Night after night they emerge in millions together. It's their only defense.
  • They climb to seek a secure perch on which to transform. Their final few weeks of their 17-years lives has just began.
  • For the first few days, the new adults rest and recover, and wait for their shells to harden.
  • Soon after the males begin to call to the females. Their synchronized chorus is among the loudest sounds in nature.
  • But not all survive long enough to find a mate.
  • Soon after the females lay eggs they punch deep holes into branches and deposits hundreds of eggs inside.
  • With that act their lives are complete, as quickly as they appear, their numbers dwindle until the last one fades away.
  • But up in the trees, hidden in the branches, billions of eggs are about to hatch.
  • They crawl to the edge of their branch, and drop away. Instinct drives them to seek refuge underground.
  • And the cycle begins again!
